## 1. Average Cost of a Single Day of On-Location Production

### Daily Production Floor Costs

Full narrative production costs range dramatically depending on budget tier:

- **Full-scale production:** $50,000–$500,000+ per day
- **Indie production (typical):** $3,000–$5,000 per day (non-union crew)
- **Micro-budget:** $1,000–$3,000 per day

### Components of Daily On-Location Production Costs

#### Crew Rates (per 12-hour workday, 2025–2026)

**Union Rates (IATSE):**
- Director of Photography: $1,000–$3,500
- Camera Operator: $600–$1,200
- Gaffer (Head Lighting): $600–$1,300
- Grips & Electricians: $300–$750 each
- Director: $1,500–$10,000+
- Producer: $2,000–$12,000+

**Non-Union Rates:**
- DP: $600–$2,000
- Camera Operator: $400–$800
- Gaffer: $300–$750
- Grips & Electricians: $150–$400 each
- Director: $500–$4,000
- Producer: $800–$5,000

#### Location-Specific Costs (per day)

- **Location rental fees:** $0–$5,000+ depending on prestige/exclusivity
- **Film permits:** $50–$500+ per location (varies by city)
- **Base camp/infrastructure:** $500–$2,000+ (trucks, trailers, craft services, parking)
- **Location catering:** $20–$50 per person per day
- **Equipment rental & transport:** Variable ($500–$5,000+)

#### Travel & Per Diem (when applicable)

- **Per diem per crew member:** $50–$150 per day (covers meals, incidentals)
- **Accommodation:** $80–$200+ per night per person
- **Travel day rate:** Half of full day rate per crew member

## 2. Average Cost of Location Scouting Per Project

### Location Scouting Service Packages

**Scouting Agency Full Project Packages:**
- Small-scale local scouting: $500–$1,500 per day
- Full project packages (includes technical scouting, permits): $2,000–$8,000 USD total
- International/complex scouting: $5,000–$8,000+

**Individual Scout/Manager Fees:**
- Commission-based: 20% of total rental amount
- Flat rate: Varies by market and experience
- Location manager on-set fee: ~$1,500+ per shoot day (AED rates ~$136 USD per location technical recce)

### Location Rental Platform Costs

- Peerspace and similar platforms: $80–$220 per hour
- Residential locations: $100–$300 per hour

### Hiring a Location Scout or Scouting Agency

**Cost Components:**

1. **Initial scouting research & compilation:** $500–$2,000
2. **Scout trip(s) travel costs:**
   - Per-person air travel: $200–$800+ depending on distance
   - Ground transportation: $50–$200 per day
   - Hotel accommodation: $80–$200+ per night
   - Meals & per diem: $50–$100 per day
   - Equipment (camera for stills/video): $200–$500

3. **Multiple scout trips:**
   - Initial scouting trip: 1–2 weeks (5–10 days typical)
   - Director/DP validation visit: 2–5 days
   - Production designer visit (optional): 2–3 days
   - Tech scout (full department): 3–5 days

4. **Permit & coordination fees:** $500–$2,000+ per location

**Total Typical Scouting Cost per Project:**
- **Indie film:** $3,000–$10,000
- **Mid-budget:** $10,000–$30,000
- **Studio production:** $30,000–$100,000+

## 3. Pre-Production Budget as % of Total Production Budget

### Industry Standard: 10–15% of Total Production Budget

**Applied across all production tiers:**
- Consistent industry standard regardless of production size
- Includes script development, casting, location scouting, crew hiring, insurance, permits

### Pre-Production Spending by Production Tier

#### Major Studio Productions ($50M+ budget)
- **Pre-production allocation:** 10–15% = $5M–$7.5M
- **Key components:** Extensive location scouting, full department pre-visualization, extensive crew hiring, comprehensive testing/R&D
- **Location scouting alone:** $50,000–$150,000+
- **Tech scout costs:** $100,000–$500,000+ (full department travel, accommodation, logistics)

#### Mid-Budget Productions ($5M–$50M budget)
- **Pre-production allocation:** 10–15% = $500,000–$7.5M
- **Location scouting:** $15,000–$50,000
- **Tech scout costs:** $20,000–$100,000
- **Multiple validation visits:** Director, DP, production designer trips included

#### Independent Productions ($500K–$5M budget)
- **Pre-production allocation:** 10–15% = $50,000–$750,000
- **Location scouting:** $5,000–$20,000
- **Tech scout costs:** $5,000–$25,000
- **Streamlined but still comprehensive process**

#### Micro-Budget Productions ($10K–$100K budget)
- **Pre-production allocation:** 10–15% = $1,000–$15,000
- **Location scouting:** Typically handled by director/producer (limited budget)
- **Tech scout:** Often minimal or informal
- **Pre-production costs:** Script development ($2,500), casting ($3,000), location scouting ($1,500), crew hiring ($6,000), insurance/permits ($1,200)

### Context: Overall Budget Breakdown

- **Above-the-Line (ATL):** ~30% of total budget
- **Below-the-Line (BTL):** ~45% of total budget
- **Production (on-set costs):** 40–45% of BTL
- **Post-production:** 10–15% of total budget

**Note:** Independent films typically exceed budgets by 40% on average; studio films exceed by 31%

## 4. Average Number of Locations Scouted vs. Used Per Production

**Industry Data:** Specific quantified ratios not readily available in public sources

### What We Know About the Process

1. **Initial compilation:** Location managers compile a list of all script-required locations
2. **Research phase:** Cross-referencing availability, permits, feasibility for each potential location
3. **Scouting trips:** Initial scout visits multiple candidate locations for each scripted location
4. **Selection phase:** Narrowing down to top 2–5 candidates per scene location
5. **Director/DP validation:** Key stakeholders visit finalists
6. **Tech scout:** Full department review 1 week before shoot (may require final refinements)
7. **Contingency locations:** Additional backups identified for weather/permitting issues

### Estimated Typical Ratio (Based on Industry Practice)

For a feature film requiring 30 primary locations:
- **Locations scouted:** 60–150+ candidates (2–5x the final number)
- **Locations shortlisted:** 40–60
- **Locations approved by director/DP:** 30–40
- **Locations finally used:** 30

**Factors affecting ratio:**
- Urban vs. remote locations (urban = more options)
- Period/specific requirements (limits options)
- Permitting availability
- Budget constraints
- Weather/season considerations

## 5. Cost of Stakeholder Coordination Meetings

### Pre-Production Coordination Meetings

**Key stakeholders typically involved:**
- Director (creative lead)
- Producer (coordinator/logistics)
- Director of Photography (visual lead)
- Production Designer (visual continuity)
- UPM (Unit Production Manager—logistics)
- Location Manager

### Meeting Cost Components

**Hourly Rate Components (per 12-hour workday rates, amortized to meeting time):**

| Role | Union Rate | Non-Union Rate |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| Director | $125–$833/hr | $42–$333/hr |
| Producer | $167–$1,000/hr | $67–$417/hr |
| DP | $83–$292/hr | $50–$167/hr |
| Production Designer | $75–$200/hr | $35–$100/hr |
| Location Manager | $50–$150/hr | $35–$100/hr |

### Typical Pre-Production Meetings

**Scouting coordination meetings:** 2–4 meetings @ 2–4 hours each
- Discussion of script requirements
- Location feasibility review
- Scout trip planning

**Cost:** $500–$3,000 per meeting (depending on team size & rates)

**Director/Producer/DP validation meeting:** 1–2 meetings @ 4–8 hours (including travel)
- On-location review with key creatives
- Travel time: 4–24 hours (travel day rates apply—typically 50% of full rate)

**Cost:** $2,000–$8,000+ per meeting/trip (includes travel, accommodation, meals)

**Full department tech scout:** 1–2 days (full crew present)
- Conducted 1 week before production
- Location walkthrough with all department heads
- Technical planning & problem-solving

**Cost:** $15,000–$50,000+ (includes travel, accommodation for 20–50+ people, meals)

## 6. Industry Data on Production Delays Caused by Location-Related Issues

### Types of Location-Related Delays

1. **Permitting delays:** Waiting for government/municipal approvals
2. **Last-minute location changes:** Venue unavailability, contractual issues, weather considerations
3. **Logistical failures:** Inadequate infrastructure, transportation issues, set-up problems
4. **Safety/regulatory issues:** Unforeseen hazards requiring location change or delay
5. **Weather/natural disasters:** Requiring location relocation (hurricanes, flooding, etc.)

### Documented Real-World Examples

**Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Man's Chest (2006)**
- Hurricane damaged key sets in Caribbean
- Required full location relocation + set rebuild
- Significant cost overruns and production schedule disruption

**The Dark Knight Rises (2012)**
- Unexpected permit delays in India
- Forced production delay while awaiting approvals
- Disrupted shooting schedule

### Industry Practice

**Contingency budgeting:** Major productions account for delays and re-shoots early in scheduling and budgets because location-related delays occur regularly.

### Cost Impact of Location Delays

**Estimated daily costs when shooting is halted due to location issues:**
- **Indie production:** $3,000–$5,000 per day (crew still paid, equipment rented)
- **Mid-budget:** $25,000–$75,000 per day
- **Studio production:** $50,000–$500,000+ per day

**Additional costs from last-minute changes:**
- New location scouting/negotiation: $1,000–$10,000+
- Replanned production logistics: $2,000–$20,000+
- Crew travel redistribution: $500–$5,000+
- Equipment re-shipment/rental: $1,000–$10,000+

**Quantified delay statistics:**
- Average Hollywood films exceed budget by 31%
- Independent films exceed budgets by 40%
- Location issues cited as a primary cause of schedule slippage

## Key Pitch Talking Points

### ROI for Shot Caller VR

**Problem:** Location scouting and production blocking consume 10–15% of pre-production budgets and require extensive travel, coordination, and crew availability.

**Cost Elimination Potential:**

1. **Scouting travel savings:** Eliminate $3,000–$30,000+ per project in scout trip costs
2. **Coordination meeting efficiency:** Reduce meeting time by 40–60% through virtual visualization
3. **Stakeholder coordination:** Eliminate need for physical validation visits (2–3 trips @ $5,000–$15,000 each)
4. **Delay risk reduction:** Reduce location-related production delays through better pre-visualization

**Conservative ROI estimate:**
- **Typical mid-budget film ($10M budget):** $1M–$1.5M pre-production budget
- **Location scouting & blocking:** $100,000–$150,000
- **Shot Caller potential savings:** $40,000–$80,000 per project (30–50% reduction)
- **Tech scout efficiency gains:** Additional $20,000–$50,000 in time/cost savings

**Aggregate market opportunity:**
- ~1,500 narrative films produced annually in US/Canada
- Average pre-production spend per film: $150,000–$300,000
- Location scouting average cost: $50,000–$75,000
- **Total addressable market: $75M–$112M annually** (in US/Canada alone)
