# Shot Caller — Judge Strategy

## Jury Criteria (from hackathon)
- Compelling Concept & High Quality Implementation
- Show what you build, not just the idea
- Filmmaking/Entertainment category: Unique immersive world model experience

## Judge-Specific Angles

| Judge | Company | Angle |
|-------|---------|-------|
| Asim Ahmed | Niantic Spatial | Real-world spatial anchoring from Maps data. Lead with location reconstruction — turning a real address into a walkable 3D space. |
| David Gene Oh | ByteDance / PICO | Pure WebSpatial. Desktop + PICO, one URL, no install. The ideal PICO WebXR use case. |
| Sherrie Cao | EA | Production workflow efficiency. Director/DP saves hours and budget on scouting and validation. |
| Ian Curtis | World Labs | Show exactly how we used Marble + SVD 360. What worked, what was hard. Be specific and honest. |
| Yiqi Zhao | Meta | Product design. Focus on how natural the location-to-validation flow feels. The UX of "being there before being there." |

## What to Emphasize Per Judge

### For Asim (Niantic) — Spatial
- The pipeline: real address → satellite/street view → SVD 360 → Gaussian splat
- Spatial accuracy and real-world grounding
- The fact that the 3D world corresponds to a real physical place

### For David (PICO) — Platform
- One URL, two modes, zero installs
- WebXR detection and seamless PICO experience
- The collaborative potential (invite stakeholders to VR walkthrough)

### For Sherrie (EA) — Business Value
- Scout trips avoided → direct cost savings
- Time to approved plan → compressed pre-production
- On-set plan changes reduced → fewer expensive surprises

### For Ian (World Labs) — Technical Depth
- How SVD 360 improves Marble input quality
- What file formats we used (.spz, .glb)
- Challenges and workarounds (splat quality, raycasting, collision mesh)

### For Yiqi (Meta) — Product Design
- The natural UX flow from address → planning → validation
- How it feels to transition from desktop to VR
- The "wow moment" of collaborative validation
