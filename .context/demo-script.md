# Shot Caller — Demo Script & Talking Points

## 90-Second Demo Script

### Act 1 — Web3D Planning Editor (laptop, ~50 seconds)
1. Show the laptop. Web3D editor open. Location input field visible.
2. Type a San Francisco address (Golden Gate Bridge area). Hit Generate.
3. Loading screen: "Generating your location..." — ~15 seconds.
4. 3D world of that real location loads. Orbit to show the space.
5. Click Camera in the sidebar. Place it on the street. FOV cone appears.
6. Click Light. Place 3-point lighting setup. Coverage cones fan out.
7. Click Cast Mark. Place actor position. Label it.
8. Hit Export. Show shareable URL and PDF floor plan.

### Act 2 — PICO VR Walkthrough (~40 seconds)
1. Hand the judge the PICO. Same URL open in PICO browser.
2. XR session launches automatically.
3. Gaussian splat world loads. Standing in the real SF location.
4. All placed elements visible at real scale — cameras, lights, marks.
5. Walk through the scene. Validate sightlines and clearances.
6. The moment: validating a real location plan with stakeholders, remotely.

## Script Talking Points

### Opening (The Problem)
"Every film shoot has the same pre-shoot bottleneck: before the crew arrives, the director, DP, AD, and gaffer need to agree on how a real space will actually work. Today that means hiring scouts, traveling to locations, coordinating in-person meetings — and every round of feedback costs another site visit. The problem isn't chaos; it's cost."

### The Solution
"Shot Caller turns that into one continuous workflow. You start with a real address or scout photos, generate a 3D reconstruction of the location, place cameras, lights, cast marks, and equipment on desktop, and then open the exact same URL on a PICO headset to walk the blocked scene at real scale."

### Why It Matters
"What matters is not just prettier 3D. It's earlier certainty. Before anyone shows up on location, the team can validate sightlines, clearances, camera coverage, lighting direction, and movement. That means fewer scout trips, faster stakeholder alignment, and a direct path from creative intent to an AD-ready plan."

### The Collaboration Angle
"Because it's a shared URL, the DP can create a playlist of candidate locations and invite the director, the camera crew, the costume team — anyone — to walk through together. No travel. No scheduling around availability. The distributed team can align on a plan even across countries."

### Closing
"Shot Caller compresses location scouting, blocking, and validation into a single workflow — so instead of figuring it out on set, crews show up with the plan already tested."

## Demo KPIs
- Address-to-world generation time
- Time-to-first-blocked-shot (camera + light + cast mark)
- VR validation latency (URL open → standing in scene)
- Export turnaround (approved scene → shareable URL/PDF)
- Scene sync reliability (desktop elements → VR accuracy)

## Product KPIs
- Planning cycle time (hours from input to approved plan)
- Physical scout trips avoided per location
- Pre-shoot issues caught per scene
- On-set plan changes per setup (downstream proof)
- Director/DP/AD approval time
- Export-to-call-sheet readiness

## Three Metrics for the Pitch
1. **Time to approved plan**
2. **Issues caught before shoot day**
3. **Scout trips avoided**

These connect directly to time, risk, and production efficiency.
